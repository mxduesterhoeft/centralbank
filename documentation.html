About the Data

The Observer contains {$Link_to_#_speeches} speeches of {$Link_to_#_central_banks} central banks.
The dataset includes several metadata, such as the name of the central bank, the time, the speaker or geographic location where it took place.
The raw speeches are obtained from central banks websites and other global repositories as FRASER or the Bank of International Settlements (BIS) website.
Please visit the Data page ({$Link_to_#_Data}) for more information on downloading speeches.

New speeches/Compilation of speeches


New speeches are added on a monthly basis.
Since there is no all encompassing repository, new speeches are obtained by comparing the new speeches of the BIS repository and the respective central bank websites.
After adding all new speeches and removing duplicates, the meta data is extracted by individual strategies depending on the source and
speech format.

For natural language processing (NLP), a pipeline of preprocessing methods is conducted.
It includes lemmatization (following the ... method), a transformation to lower case, removing of stoppwords, punctuation and numbers.

For information extraction, we conduct several measurements:

  1. Sentiment indicators are computed based on different dictionaries and weighting methods. Those include Loughran, McDonald (2012) and Blix-Grimaldi (...).
  2. Textual complexity measures are extracted on the basis of () and ().
  3. We conduct several topic models following the classic LDA method (Blei et al 2003). The exact parameter settings can be found here. We don't predict topics on new documents, but estimating the whole corpus again.

    Finally, all new speeches are indexed via ElasticSearch and deployed on Kibana.

  • Year Range: 1990 - today ($Link_Date_of_last_Speech)
  • We provide the raw speech in its original format, which can be either html (text) or pdf. The pdf format contains mainly machine readable text. Other cases include
    pictures of old documents, which is not directly accessible. We recommend optical character recognition by software or simply by using adobe readers text recognition
    option.

Limitations and missing data

  • Some swedish speeches (Norwegian) are only available on youtube. Those are not included in the corpus.
  • Some of the speeches are only available in their native language. If no official translation could be found, we
    translated them via ("google translate" and ""). Automated translation is far from being perfect and limits usuability of those documents regarding certain text mining methods.
    For a discussion of translation services in the use of social science, see here (Roberts 2015?).
  • No bibliographic search is available yet. We excluded the bibliography for textual analysis, but the raw documents still contain those.
